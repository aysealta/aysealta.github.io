About

A technical path shaped by real-world systems.

I’m a Computer Engineer with a background in electrical and electronics engineering, currently working in IT and web systems.

My interest in technology has always been driven by real-world impact. I’ve long been drawn to biomedical engineering and the role technology can play in improving human health and accessibility. That perspective shaped the way I think about systems today—not just as things to build, but as things that need to work reliably in real environments.

In my current role, I work across multiple web platforms and internal tools, focusing on system performance, reliability, and day-to-day operations. I also design coding challenges for international competitions, which keeps me closely connected to algorithmic thinking and problem-solving.

Over time, I became increasingly interested in what happens behind the scenes—how systems are deployed, maintained, and scaled. That curiosity naturally led me toward DevOps.

My long-term goal is to bring together this systems perspective, my growing interest in DevOps, and my curiosity about biomedical and data-driven applications to contribute to technologies that are both innovative and dependable in real-world use.
